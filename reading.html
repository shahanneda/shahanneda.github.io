Elon Musk by Walter Isaacson

Exciting read. It was very inspirational seeing how close Elon was to failure at many times. The biggest takeaway for me is how Elon always lives his life with a mission, and inspires others with that mission as well. The mission being "accelerate the worlds transition to EV's" and "make humans multiplanatory". I think having a mission that leads you is extremely powerful.

Steve Jobs by Walter Isaacson

I never knew that Steve had such an interesting past. The story of him getting kicked out of apple and then starting NeXT and Pixar was also inspiring. Similar to Elon he also had a mission, to make great products that change everything. The fact that they did not do any product research for the iPhone as opposed to most other products was interesting. Had a few tears in my eyes after some points, especially after going back and watching the product launches and the 1984 commercial.

Sapians

I loved this book. I often wonder about how amazing the world around us is, and how us humans have built all this. Sapians really went through this whole process, all the way from prehistoric times and other homo species, to culture and different economic and political systems. The whole idea of everything being a common fiction that we all believe in was impactful, for example companies and banks. The effectiveness of capitalism was a highlight and how instead of human depending on family and close friends for things, now we depend on markets and money. It's amazing that it actually works. The rapid change in the last 100 years makes me excited to see what the next 100 years leads to, and I'm glad to be alive now. It really is an amazing time to be alive. The last part on the future of humanity especially with genetic engineering was also insightful.

Player of Games by Ian Banks

What a wild ride. This book made me excited about the future. The flying drones and have AGI and help humans was fascinating and exciting. Living a multiplanitary life was also inspiring. Another takeaway was that having life dictated by a game only and solely playing to win based on the game is not a good idea.

The Foundations

I didn't enjoy the short stories aspect of this book. Everytime I actually started to really enjoy the story it ended and a new one started. I really liked the first three though, haven't read the last one yet. The whole idea of psychohistory, using math to predict the future, was pretty cool, so I will say that. Also note that this book was way ahead of its time.

Siddhartha

On the shorter side, but a really impactful story. I enjoyed the his journey and the different stages of his life and related to him a bit a well.
